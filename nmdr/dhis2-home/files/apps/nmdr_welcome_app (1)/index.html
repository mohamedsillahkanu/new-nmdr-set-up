Sierra Leone NMCP

National Malaria Data Repository

A centralized, integrated platform for malaria surveillance, programme monitoring, and data-driven decision making across Sierra Leone.

About the Repository

01 — Introduction
What is the NMDR?
  • A national digital infrastructure for storing, managing and accessing malaria programme data
  • Hosted on DHIS2 and integrated with HMIS, LMIS and surveillance systems
  • Developed through collaboration between NMCP, ICF-SL, WHO, AHADI and CHAI
  • Designed to support the full subnational tailoring (SNT) pipeline
02 — Purpose
Why the NMDR Exists
  • Consolidate fragmented malaria data from multiple sources into one authoritative repository
  • Eliminate data silos between HMIS, LMIS, surveys and surveillance reporting
  • Provide a single source of truth for malaria indicators used in planning and reviews
  • Strengthen national data governance and reporting standards
03 — Focus Areas
Key Programme Areas
  • Case management — testing, treatment and reporting across public, community and private sectors
  • Vector control — IRS coverage, ITN distribution and utilisation tracking
  • Malaria in pregnancy — IPTp, ANC attendance and maternal outcomes
  • Epidemiology — incidence, mortality, TPR and parasite prevalence by district
04 — Benefits
Expected Benefits
  • Faster, evidence-based programme planning through readily accessible historical data
  • Improved data quality through built-in validation, completeness checks and DQA tools
  • Enhanced transparency and accountability in malaria indicator reporting
  • Strengthened capacity for subnational tailoring and targeted intervention delivery
